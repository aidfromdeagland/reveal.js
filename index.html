Core values

Do you know what this prophet holds in his hands??? →→→
So, it is Manifesto for AGILE Software Development where 4 essential values of AGILE are declared. →→→

Core values

  • Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
  • Working software over comprehensive documentation
  • Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
  • Responding to change over following a plan
And these values say, that: →→→
• The focus should be more on individuals and interactions instead of processes and tools. →→→
• Working software is more important than comprehensive documentation →→→
• Customer collaboration is more vital than contract negotiation →→→
• The process should respond to change rather than follow a plan
I think, nothing of this requires my explanation. One thing, I want to point out - these statements are not about one item exclude or substitute another. No, it's about priority. For example, actual and detailed documentation - is a great thing, that helps during the development process, post-release support, refactoring, and so on. But when we choose, what to do first: usable product or perfect documentation, we prefer the product. →→→

Main principles

  • Our highest priority is to satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software.
  • Welcome changing requirements, even late in development. AGILE processes harness change for the customer's competitive advantage.
  • Deliver working software frequently, from a couple of weeks to a couple of months, with a preference to the shorter timescale.
  • Business people and developers must work together daily throughout the project.
  • Build projects around motivated individuals. Give them the environment and support they need, and trust them to get the job done.
  • The most efficient and effective method of conveying information to and within a development team is face-to-face conversation.
  • Working software is the primary measure of progress.
  • AGILE processes promote sustainable development. The sponsors, developers, and users should be able to maintain a constant pace indefinitely.
  • Continuous attention to technical excellence and good design enhances agility.
  • Simplicity - the art of maximizing the amount of work not done - is essential.
  • The best architectures, requirements, and designs emerge from self-organizing teams.
  • At regular intervals, the team reflects on how to become more effective, then tunes and adjusts its behavior accordingly.
In addition to the values we have twelve principles of AGILE. →→→ Each of them is important and all of them together are about communication, collaboration, high-qualified and cross-functional team, personal responsibility and reacting to changing environment. →→→

Let’s compare AGILE with a traditional approach – also known as WATERFALL. →→→

AGILE VS WATERFALL

AGILE WATERFALL
Organizational structure iterative linear
User requirements interactive input clearly defined before implementation
Involvement of clients high low
Development model evolutionary delivery life cycle
Model preference favors adaption favors plan
Product or process less focus on formal and directive processes more serious about processes than the product
Effort estimation the team does the estimation PM provides estimates and gets approval from PO for the entire project
Reviews and approvals reviews are done after each iteration excessive reviews and approvals by leaders
We have a comparative table. Let's go to each criteria. →→→
Organizational structure: iterative and linear

An iteration, in the context of an AGILE project, is a timebox during which development takes place. Usually an iteration takes from 1 to 4 weeks. An iteration is a kind of full-cycle mini-project, that includes all stages of development process with some part of functionality: from planing and design to testing and delivery. When an iteration ends, a new iterations begins with a new part of the functionality to implement and all process repeats. If some tasks were failed, they moved to the next iteration.
In WATERFALL model we have a linear structure, where every next step starts only when previous was fully completed. There are no iterations or partial implementations and our client gets his product at the very end. ↓

User requirements and involvement of clients
Permanent input of user requirements with frequent delivery of the product in AGILE provides a high level of client involvement and makes connections between clients and developers closer.
When in WATERFALL model the main source of client’s requirements – is requirements specification as an appendix to the contract. And if this specification is not detailed enough, the final product may not meet client's expectations ↓
model preference and business processes
Taking into account high uncertainty, AGILE can't set strict processes and rules, because new requirements may need new processes and new models of interaction. Adaption becomes a key feature of business processes
In WATERFALL model, business processes are more formal and more bureaucracy takes place. ↓
effort estimation
Because of the high qualification and experience of AGILE team, they make effort estimation by themselves. Effort estimation takes place at the beginning of each iteration and usually measured in so-called story points.
In WATERFALL the only person, who provides estimates is Project Manager. Efforts are estimated in hours ↓
Finally, the last point - reviews. In AGILE reviews take place at the end of each iteration, when team members discuss what was good, what was bad, what kind of changes they need to implement in processes, etc… The team interested in this kind of review because it helps to make interactions better and whole job more comfortable.
Reviews in terms of WATERFALL model may take features of routine reporting for the customer. →→→

Benefits

As you can see, AGILE is very different from a traditional approach. And these differences give us the next benefits. →→→

flexibility

FLEXIBILITY
When it comes to making changes in the product or a process, AGILE is much more flexible than WATERFALL. AGILE focuses on the product and if our customer needs changes, we have no problem to tune all processes or change the product to meet updated requirements. ↓
TRANSPARENCY
AGILE keeps processes transparent. The clients, team members, and management are actively involved during the development lifecycle. All team members see the progress right from the start to the end. Everyone knows what another does. All is clear and transparent. It makes interactions more effective. ↓

ownership and accountability

OWNERSHIP AND ACCOUNTABILITY
In the AGILE methodology, every team member shares ownership of the project. Each one of them plays an active role to complete the iteration within the estimated time. High personal responsibility ensures more quality products. ↓
FEEDBACK
AGILE welcomes feedback that is helpful in providing better products. The team can respond to customer requests and deliver a high-quality product, that meets the actual requirements. ↓

handling with complex projects

HANDLING WITH COMPLEX PROJECTS
Because of the high collaboration and cross-functionality of the team, they can handle more complex projects with many interdependencies and factors. →→→
